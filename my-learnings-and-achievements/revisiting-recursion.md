## Revisiting Recursion

Today was my second time studying recursion, and it felt very different from the first.

Instead of just trying to solve problems, I spent time understanding the patterns behind them. I worked through problems like Combination Sum, Subsets, Generate Parentheses, and Letter Combinations, and I finally started seeing how many of them are built on just a few backtracking ideas.

One thing that stood out today was how small changes in recursion—like deciding whether to stay on the same index, move to the next one, or skip duplicates—completely change the problem you're solving.

I'm also documenting every problem and the intuition behind it in my GitHub. It's taking a little longer, but I know future me will be grateful for those notes.

Still a long way to go, but days like this make the journey feel worthwhile.
